The active component of the drug Ad-Linco is lincomycin hydrochloride, 4.4% w/v. Acne vulgaris is a typical skin ailment that is treated with lincomycin, an antibiotic from the lincosamide family. Alcohol, propylene glycol, methylparaben, propylparaben, and purified water are among the inactive components found in Ad-Linco. These inert components aid in forming the solution and guarantee its effectiveness and stability. Ad-Linco is a topical medication that is applied straight to the skin's afflicted regions. It functions by preventing acne-causing bacteria like Propionibacterium acnes from proliferating and growing. By attaching to the bacterial ribosome, lincomycin stops the production of proteins required for bacterial growth and reproduction. Ad-Linco can help to lessen the amount of acne-causing bacteria on the skin by preventing bacterial growth, which can help to lessen the look of acne. Ad-Linco should be taken as prescribed by your doctor, who may advise applying a tiny amount of solution to the skin's affected area once or twice a day. When applying the treatment, it's crucial to prevent getting any of the solution in your eyes, nose, or mouth and to wash your hands completely. Ad-Linco may result in side effects, much like all drugs. Ad-Linco frequently causes skin irritability, redness, dryness, and peeling. Within a few days of the beginning of the treatment, these side effects are typically minor and disappear on their own. Although they are uncommon, more severe side effects could include allergic reactions including hives or breathing problems. Even if your acne seems to have improved, it is crucial to use Ad-Linco exactly as prescribed by your doctor and to finish the entire course of treatment. If the full course of treatment is not followed, the acne may return or develop a drug resistance. Overall, Ad-Linco is a topical solution intended to treat acne vulgaris that has lincomycin hydrochloride 4.4% w/v as its active component. It functions by preventing acne-causing bacteria on the skin from growing and reproducing.
